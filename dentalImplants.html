--Root Canal Treatment + Full Ceramic (Metal free) Crown = Rs. 10,000

-- In case of badly broken tooth, a post may be required = Rs. 1,000

           After the dental implant has healed, there’s often one more surgical step — placing a small attachment, called an abutment or collar, on the top of the implant.
           The abutment usually requires local anesthesia.
           For some patients, the abutment might be attached during the original implant procedure, but that means the abutment will be visible above the gumline throughout the long healing time. Many patients don’t like how that looks, so it’s placed later on instead. Either way, the abutment attaches to the metal post of the implant, and the gum tissue is closed around it.
           This requires about two weeks of healing for your gums before the final replacement tooth or teeth can be placed.

           After that, impressions will be taken for the creation of your new artificial tooth. These new teeth may be either removable or fixed; your dentist will consult with you about what’s best for your case.

           Finally, your new crowns or replacement teeth will be attached to the abutments and you’ll be ready to face the world with a new smile. It may take some time to adjust to the replacement tooth, so follow your dentist’s aftercare instructions closely to ensure everything goes well.

           Over the next several weeks to months, the implant undergoes a process called osseointegration, where it fuses with the surrounding bone tissue. This provides a stable foundation for the prosthetic tooth or teeth.

What to expect in a teeth whitening session_

           After examining your teeth, our dentist will recommend the best treatment. We will measure the original shade of your teeth against the results after the treatment. The dental whitening gel is applied to the teeth and left on for the length of time prescribed. With laser teeth lightening, a special light activates the gel to provide faster results. After the gel is removed and rinsed away, you’ll see a noticeable difference of three to eight shades whiter.

          To get quicker results from teeth whitening, you have to use a stronger strength of treatment which can only be offered at a dentist’s office. If you use a do-it-yourself kit at home, your results can take much longer, sometimes months, to see a noticeable difference. Talk with our dentist to see if this is the right option for you.

          You can brush your teeth before and after using teeth whitening strips. However, be careful not to irritate your gums, as they can be sensitive to the topical gel. It’s best to check with a dentist before you choose at-home teeth whitening strips because the dentist can see if you have any other teeth issues that may impact or interfere with the procedure.
