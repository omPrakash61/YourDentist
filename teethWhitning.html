           Teeth whitening is one of the most popular cosmetic dentistry treatments offering a quick, non-invasive and affordable way to enhance a smile. Universally valued by men and women alike, whitening (or bleaching) treatments are available to satisfy every budget, time frame and temperament.

        Whether in the form of professionally administered one-hour whitening sessions at a dental office or cosmetic spa, or home-use bleaching kits purchased at your local drugstore, solutions abound. Virtually everyone who opts for a teeth whitening solution sees moderate to substantial improvement in the brightness and whiteness of their smile.

         That said, it’s not a permanent solution to discoloration and requires maintenance or “touch-ups” for a prolonged effect. In this article we break down everything related to teeth whitening, including the process of tooth discoloration, what causes staining, the various treatment options available, and their associated risks and costs

What to expect in a teeth whitening session_

           After examining your teeth, our dentist will recommend the best treatment. We will measure the original shade of your teeth against the results after the treatment. The dental whitening gel is applied to the teeth and left on for the length of time prescribed. With laser teeth lightening, a special light activates the gel to provide faster results. After the gel is removed and rinsed away, you’ll see a noticeable difference of three to eight shades whiter.

          To get quicker results from teeth whitening, you have to use a stronger strength of treatment which can only be offered at a dentist’s office. If you use a do-it-yourself kit at home, your results can take much longer, sometimes months, to see a noticeable difference. Talk with our dentist to see if this is the right option for you.

          You can brush your teeth before and after using teeth whitening strips. However, be careful not to irritate your gums, as they can be sensitive to the topical gel. It’s best to check with a dentist before you choose at-home teeth whitening strips because the dentist can see if you have any other teeth issues that may impact or interfere with the procedure.
